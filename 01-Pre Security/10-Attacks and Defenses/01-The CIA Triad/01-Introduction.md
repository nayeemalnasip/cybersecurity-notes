# Cyber Security Fundamentals — Study Note

## Introduction to the Pillars of Cyber Security

### Overview

Cyber security is the practice of protecting digital systems, networks, applications, and data from unauthorized access, attacks, damage, or disruption. As organizations increasingly rely on digital technologies, protecting information assets has become a critical responsibility.

At the core of cyber security lies a foundational model known as the **CIA Triad**, which represents the three primary objectives that security professionals aim to protect:

```text
Confidentiality
Integrity
Availability
```

These three principles serve as the foundation for designing, implementing, and evaluating security controls across modern information systems.

---

## Main Concept

### What is it?

Cyber security focuses on protecting valuable digital assets by ensuring that information remains:

* Private from unauthorized users
* Accurate and trustworthy
* Accessible when needed

These objectives are represented by the CIA Triad:

| Principle       | Purpose                                           |
| --------------- | ------------------------------------------------- |
| Confidentiality | Protect information from unauthorized access      |
| Integrity       | Ensure information remains accurate and unchanged |
| Availability    | Ensure systems and data remain accessible         |

Together, these principles guide security decisions and risk management strategies.

---

## How It Works

### Step 1: Protect Confidentiality

Sensitive information should only be accessible to authorized individuals.

Examples:

```text
Passwords
Banking Information
Medical Records
Business Documents
```

Security controls include:

```text
Encryption
Access Control
Authentication
Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA)
```

---

### Step 2: Maintain Integrity

Information must remain accurate, complete, and unaltered.

The goal is to prevent:

```text
Unauthorized Modifications
Data Tampering
Data Corruption
```

Security controls include:

```text
Hashing
Digital Signatures
Version Control
Audit Logs
```

---

### Step 3: Ensure Availability

Authorized users must be able to access systems and information whenever required.

Threats include:

```text
Hardware Failures
System Crashes
Denial-of-Service Attacks
Power Outages
```

Security controls include:

```text
Backups
Redundant Systems
Load Balancing
Disaster Recovery Plans
```

---

## Important Components

The practice of protecting digital assets from threats and attacks.

Examples:

```text
Networks
Servers
```

---

### Confidentiality

Protecting information from unauthorized disclosure.

Example:

```text
Only authorized employees can access payroll records.
```

---

Ensuring information remains accurate and trustworthy.

Example:


---

### Availability

Ensuring information and services remain accessible.

Example:

```text
An online banking service remains operational 24/7.
```

---

### CIA Triad

The three core pillars of information security:

```text
C = Confidentiality
I = Integrity
A = Availability
```

This framework is used globally to evaluate security measures.
