# Cyber Security Fundamentals — Study Note

## Understanding the CIA Triad

### Overview

In today's digital world, vast amounts of sensitive information are stored, processed, and transmitted electronically. This information includes personal records, financial transactions, government documents, healthcare data, and business information. Protecting this data is one of the primary goals of cyber security.

Cyber security revolves around three fundamental principles known as the **CIA Triad**:

```text
Confidentiality
Integrity
Availability
```

These three pillars define what information security aims to protect and serve as the foundation for security policies, technologies, and risk management practices.

---

## Main Concept

### What is the CIA Triad?

The CIA Triad is a security framework that defines the three primary objectives of information security.

| Principle       | Purpose                                     |
| --------------- | ------------------------------------------- |
| Confidentiality | Protect data from unauthorized access       |
| Integrity       | Protect data from unauthorized modification |
| Availability    | Ensure data and services remain accessible  |

Every security control, policy, or defense mechanism ultimately supports one or more of these principles.

---

## How It Works

### Step 1: Protect Confidentiality

Sensitive information should only be accessible to authorized individuals.

Goal:

```text
Prevent Unauthorized Access
Prevent Information Disclosure
Protect Privacy
```

Examples:

* User account passwords
* Medical records
* Financial information
* Company secrets

Security Controls:

```text
Encryption
Access Control
Authentication
Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA)
Permissions Management
```

---

### Step 2: Maintain Integrity

Data must remain accurate, complete, and trustworthy.

Goal:

```text
Prevent Unauthorized Modification
Prevent Data Tampering
Maintain Data Accuracy
```

Examples:

* Student grades
* Banking transactions
* Attendance records
* Business reports

Security Controls:

```text
Hashing
Digital Signatures
Checksums
Audit Logs
Version Control
```

---

### Step 3: Ensure Availability

Authorized users must be able to access systems and information when required.

Goal:

```text
Prevent Service Interruptions
Maintain Business Operations
Ensure Continuous Access
```

Examples:

* Online banking systems
* Company websites
* Email services
* Healthcare systems

Security Controls:

```text
Backups
Redundant Systems
Disaster Recovery Plans
Load Balancing
Power Generators
```

---

## Important Components

### Confidentiality

Confidentiality focuses on protecting information from unauthorized disclosure.

Real-World Example:

```text
Having a private conversation in a secure location
instead of discussing sensitive information publicly.
```

Digital Example:

```text
Using encrypted connections when logging into
social media accounts over public networks.
```

---

### Integrity

Integrity ensures information remains accurate and unchanged unless modified by authorized individuals.

Real-World Example:

```text
A teacher verifies student grades before
submitting them to examination authorities.
```

Digital Example:

```text
Ensuring banking transaction details are not altered
during transmission.
```

---

### Availability

Availability ensures systems and information remain accessible when needed.

Real-World Example:

```text
A bank installing backup generators to continue
operations during power outages.
```

Digital Example:

```text
A website using traffic management systems to
remain operational during heavy traffic or attacks.
```

---

### CIA Triad Relationship

The three principles work together:

```text
Confidentiality → Protect Privacy
Integrity       → Protect Accuracy
Availability    → Protect Accessibility
```

A secure system should maintain all three simultaneously.

---

## Advantages / Benefits

* Protects sensitive information.
* Maintains trust in digital systems.
* Supports business continuity.
* Reduces security risks.
* Prevents unauthorized activities.
* Ensures reliable access to services.
* Forms the foundation of cybersecurity strategies.

## Key Characteristics

* Confidentiality protects secrecy.
* Integrity protects correctness.
* Availability protects accessibility.
* All three principles are equally important.
* Security controls often support multiple CIA principles.
* Most cyber attacks target one or more CIA components.
* The CIA Triad is a globally recognized security model.
