# What is Networking?

## Overview

Networking is the concept of connecting devices or systems so they can communicate and share information. In computing, networks can range from two connected devices to billions of interconnected systems worldwide.

## Objectives

* Understand the basic concept of networking.
* Learn how networks exist in both everyday life and computing.
* Recognize the importance of networking in cybersecurity.
* Identify examples of devices and systems that rely on networks.

## Methodology / Steps

### Understanding Networks

A network is a collection of connected entities that can communicate or interact with one another.

### Real-World Network Examples

Networks exist in many areas of daily life, including:

* Public transportation systems
* National power grids
* Social connections between people
* Postal and parcel delivery systems

### Networking in Computing

In computing, networks connect devices to enable communication and data exchange.

Examples of connected devices include:

* Laptops
* Smartphones
* Security cameras
* Traffic lights
* Agricultural systems

### Network Scale

Networks can vary greatly in size:

* Small networks consisting of two devices
* Large networks containing billions of connected devices

### Importance in Cybersecurity

Networking supports critical services such as:

* Weather data collection
* Electricity distribution
* Traffic management

Understanding networking is essential because cybersecurity relies heavily on how devices communicate and exchange information.

## Key Findings / Notes

* A network consists of connected devices or systems.
* Networks exist in both physical and digital environments.
* Computing networks enable communication and data sharing.
* Networks can range from small local connections to global infrastructures.
* Networking is a fundamental concept in cybersecurity.
