# What is Internet?

## Overview

The Internet is a global network composed of many smaller interconnected networks. These networks communicate with each other to enable the exchange of information and resources across the world.

---

## Objectives

- Understand the concept of the Internet.
- Learn how multiple networks connect to form the Internet.
- Explore the history of the Internet.
- Differentiate between private and public networks.

## Methodology / Steps

### 1. Understanding the Internet

The Internet is not a single network. It is a collection of many smaller networks connected together to allow communication between devices worldwide.

### 2. Network Expansion Example

In the provided example:

- Alice can communicate with Bob and Jim.
- Alice can also communicate with Zayn and Toby.
- Since Alice understands both groups, she acts as a bridge between them.
- This creates a larger interconnected network.

This demonstrates how separate networks can be connected through intermediary systems.

### 3. History of the Internet

#### ARPANET (Late 1960s)

- First documented computer network.
- Developed under the ARPANET project.
- Funded by the United States Department of Defense.

#### World Wide Web (1989)

- Invented by Tim Berners-Lee.
- Introduced the World Wide Web (WWW).
- Enabled information storage and sharing on a global scale.

### 4. Internet Structure

The Internet consists of:

- Multiple small networks
- Interconnected communication paths
- Devices exchanging information across networks

### 5. Types of Networks

#### Private Network

A network that operates within a limited environment and connects local devices.

#### Public Network

A network that connects multiple private networks together. The Internet is an example of a public network.

### 6. Device Identification

Devices use unique labels or identifiers to distinguish themselves from other devices on a network and facilitate communication.
