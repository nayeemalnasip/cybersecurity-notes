# Computer Types

## Summary of Computer Types

### Overview

Computers exist in many forms and are designed to meet different requirements. While many people think of computers as devices with screens and keyboards, computing technology extends far beyond traditional desktops and laptops. Modern society relies on a wide variety of computing systems, many of which operate silently in the background without direct user interaction.

This section summarizes the different computer types covered throughout the room and reinforces the concept that computer design is driven by purpose, performance requirements, reliability needs, and operational environments.

## How It Works

### Computer Specialization

Rather than creating one universal computer, manufacturers design systems based on intended use.

Process:

```text
Purpose
   ↓
Design Requirements
   ↓
Hardware Selection
   ↓
Computer Type
```

Different requirements lead to different computer designs.

Examples:

```text
Mobility      → Smartphone
Performance   → Workstation
Reliability   → Server
Automation    → IoT Device
Control       → Embedded System
```

## Key Characteristics

* Computers exist in many different forms.
* Not all computers have screens or keyboards.
* Different systems are designed for different purposes.
* Servers provide services to other devices.
* IoT devices communicate over networks.
* Embedded systems operate inside larger devices.
* Performance, mobility, and reliability often require trade-offs.
* Many critical systems operate silently in the background.

## Key Notes

* Computers are far more common than most people realize.
* Many devices contain hidden computing systems.
* Specialized computer designs exist because no single system is ideal for every task.
* Servers, IoT devices, and embedded systems play critical roles in modern infrastructure.
* Embedded systems often operate for years without direct user awareness.
* Critical services depend on reliable computing systems working behind the scenes.
* Cybersecurity professionals must understand all types of computer systems because each introduces unique security challenges and attack surfaces.
